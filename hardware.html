Computer Hardware

Motherboard- What is a motherboard and what does it do?

The motherboard is a main piece of a computer that holds together many key components. The bottom of it is made of a firm, non-conductive material, usually plastic. Traces, thin layers of copper or aluminum foil are printed onto the plastic; these contain circuits, which allow the computer to work.


CPU- What is a CPU and what does it do?

The CPU, also known as the “brains” of a computer, stands for the central processing unit, which inputs the instructions given from the keyboard, mouse, or other output, processes it, and tells the rest of the computer what to do. It is on the motherboard and has tiny fans and heatsinks to keep it from overheating. In addition to computers, it’s found in almost any electronic device, including washing machines, phones, and dvd players.

RAM- What is a RAM and what does it do?

RAM stands for random access memory, which means that it is storage that is lost when the power is lost. ROM, on the other hand, stands for read-only memory, which means that it is permanently stored data- once it’s written on, it can only be read, not edited. This however, holds memory even when there’s no power.
